# Additive Project References

These six papers were selected because they align directly with the additive public-opinion-shaping mainline: classical opinion dynamics and interventions, quantum strategic encoding, and amplitude-estimation-based observable payoff evaluation.

## Downloaded Papers

1. **Morris H. DeGroot (1974), _Reaching a Consensus_**
   - File: `DeGroot_1974_Reaching_a_Consensus.pdf`
   - Source: <https://www.cse.cuhk.edu.hk/~cslui/CMSC5734/DeGroot-Reaching_a_concensus.pdf>
   - Why it matters: foundational linear opinion-dynamics model; directly motivates the additive state-update view with shared public-opinion coordinates and repeated mixing.

2. **Anish Gaitonde, Jon Kleinberg, Eva Tardos (2020), _Adversarial Perturbations of Opinion Dynamics in Networks_**
   - File: `Gaitonde_Kleinberg_Tardos_2020_Adversarial_Perturbations_of_Opinion_Dynamics_in_Networks.pdf`
   - Source: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/2003.07010.pdf>
   - Why it matters: models strategic perturbations to opinion dynamics under network interaction; closely related to external influence allocation and payoff distortion under intervention.

3. **Luca Damonte, Giacomo Como, Fabio Fagnani (2022), _Targeting interventions for displacement minimization in opinion dynamics_**
   - File: `Damonte_Como_Fagnani_2022_Targeting_Interventions_for_Displacement_Minimization_in_Opinion_Dynamics.pdf`
   - Source: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/2209.06481.pdf>
   - Why it matters: directly studies intervention design in opinion-dynamics systems; relevant to your additive setup where multiple agents allocate influence to move the terminal distribution.

4. **David A. Meyer (1998), _Quantum strategies_**
   - File: `Meyer_1998_Quantum_Strategies.pdf`
   - Source: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/quant-ph/9804010.pdf>
   - Why it matters: one of the foundational references for quantum strategic representations; useful when positioning the quantum-encoded game as a strategic encoding rather than a different payoff model.

5. **J. Eisert, M. Wilkens, M. Lewenstein (1998), _Quantum Games and Quantum Strategies_**
   - File: `Eisert_Wilkens_Lewenstein_1998_Quantum_Games_and_Quantum_Strategies.pdf`
   - Source: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/quant-ph/9806088.pdf>
   - Why it matters: canonical quantum-game formalism; strong citation support for the claim that quantum representations can preserve or alter strategic structure depending on encoding.

6. **Gilles Brassard, Peter Hoyer, Michele Mosca, Alain Tapp (2000), _Quantum Amplitude Amplification and Estimation_**
   - File: `Brassard_Hoyer_Mosca_Tapp_2000_Quantum_Amplitude_Amplification_and_Estimation.pdf`
   - Source: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/quant-ph/0005055.pdf>
   - Why it matters: the core theoretical reference for your observable-payoff estimation benchmark and the sample-complexity discussion around amplitude estimation versus Monte Carlo.

## Suggested Citation Roles In Your Deck / Report

- **Classical grounding:** DeGroot (1974), Gaitonde et al. (2020), Damonte et al. (2022)
- **Quantum strategic encoding:** Meyer (1998), Eisert et al. (1998)
- **Observable estimation / query advantage:** Brassard et al. (2000)
